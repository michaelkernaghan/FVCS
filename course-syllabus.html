Introduction to Croquet

6-Session Beginner Course Syllabus

Course Overview

This comprehensive introduction to croquet provides adults and seniors with the knowledge and skills to enjoy this timeless sport. Over six 1.5-hour sessions, participants will learn the history, varieties, health benefits, and fundamental techniques of croquet.

Duration: 6 sessions, 1.5 hours each
Level: Beginner to Intermediate
Equipment: Provided by FVCS
Age: Adults and Seniors

Session-by-Session Curriculum

Session 1: Introduction & History

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • History of Croquet: Origins, evolution, and cultural significance of the game
  • Game Varieties: Introduction to Golf Croquet, Association Croquet, and recreational variants
  • Health Benefits: Physical activity, social engagement, mental stimulation, and outdoor recreation
  • Equipment Overview: Court layout, hoops, mallets, balls, and accessories
  • Basic Rules: Fundamental concepts and game objectives

Activities:

  • Introduction and welcome
  • Course expectations and safety guidelines
  • Demonstration of basic game play
  • Initial hands-on experience with equipment

Session 2: Mallet Selection & Basic Technique

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • Mallet Selection: Types, weights, lengths, and personal fitting
  • Grip Techniques: Standard grip, variations, and hand positioning
  • Stance: Proper body alignment, foot placement, and balance
  • Swing Fundamentals: Pendulum motion, follow-through, and power control
  • Ball Contact: Sweet spot, angle of approach, and common errors

Activities:

  • Individual mallet fitting and selection
  • Basic stroke practice drills
  • Target practice exercises
  • Peer observation and feedback

Session 3: Ball Control & Hoop Running

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • Basic Ball Control: Direction, distance, and accuracy
  • Hoop Running Techniques: Angles, approach, and execution
  • Stop Shots: Controlling ball after contact
  • Court Positioning: Reading the court and planning shots
  • Practice Strategies: Effective solo and group practice methods

Activities:

  • Accuracy drills to marked targets
  • Hoop running practice from various distances
  • Distance control exercises
  • Mini challenges and games

Session 4: Ball-to-Ball Shots & Roqueting

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • Roqueting: Striking another ball and its tactical purposes
  • Ball-to-Ball Accuracy: Aiming, contact point, and follow-through
  • Croquet Shots: Introduction to taking croquet after a roquet
  • Shot Planning: Thinking ahead and strategic positioning
  • Game Tactics: When to attack, defend, or reposition

Activities:

  • Roqueting practice at varying distances
  • Basic croquet shot exercises
  • Tactical scenario discussions
  • Paired practice drills

Session 5: Golf Croquet - Rules & Play

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • Golf Croquet Rules: Complete rules, scoring, and turn order
  • Strategy: Offensive and defensive tactics
  • Court Etiquette: Proper behavior, turn sequence, and sportsmanship
  • Game Management: Keeping score, calling faults, and timing
  • Match Play: Singles and doubles formats

Activities:

  • First full Golf Croquet game with instruction
  • Rotating partners for multiple match experience
  • Real-time coaching and feedback
  • Discussion of game situations and decisions

Session 6: Practice Games & Next Steps

1.5 hours

Topics Covered:

  • Review & Refinement: Addressing common challenges and questions
  • Advanced Techniques: Introduction to jump shots, split shots, and advanced tactics
  • Game Variations: Snake Croquet, Ricochet Croquet, and other variants
  • Continuing Your Croquet Journey: Club play, practice resources, and skill development
  • Course Feedback: Participant experience and suggestions

Activities:

  • Supervised practice games
  • Individual skill assessment and feedback
  • Introduction to FVCS member clubs
  • Certificate of completion
  • Information on ongoing play opportunities

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this 6-session course, participants will be able to:

Knowledge

  • Understand the history and varieties of croquet
  • Explain the rules of Golf Croquet
  • Recognize proper equipment and court setup
  • Identify tactical situations and strategies

Skills

  • Execute proper stance, grip, and swing technique
  • Control ball direction and distance effectively
  • Run hoops from various positions
  • Successfully roquet other balls
  • Play a complete Golf Croquet game

Confidence

  • Feel comfortable on the croquet court
  • Join club play sessions
  • Practice independently
  • Teach basic concepts to others
  • Continue skill development

Important Information

What to Bring

  • Comfortable outdoor clothing
  • Closed-toe shoes with flat soles
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
  • Water bottle
  • Notebook (optional, for personal notes)

What's Provided

  • All croquet equipment (mallets, balls, hoops)
  • Professional instruction
  • Course materials and handouts
  • Use of croquet court
  • Certificate of completion

Prerequisites

  • No prior croquet experience required
  • Ability to walk and stand for extended periods
  • Ability to bend and swing a mallet
  • Suitable for all fitness levels
